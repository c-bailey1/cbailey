Gravlax with Dill, Mustard & Lemon

Home Page Ingredients Making Dough Folding & Cutting Cooking Pasta Pasta Story Gravlax Recipe

Gravlax is a Scandinavian dish made from raw salmon that has been cured, not cooked, using a mixture of salt, sugar, and dill.

The word gravlax is Swedish, meaning buried salmon, which refers to the traditional method of curing the fish underground.

Ingredients

Ingredient Amount
Salmon fillet, skin-on, pin bones removed 1 lb
Kosher salt 2 tbsp
Raw sugar 1 tbsp
Fennel seeds 1 tsp
Cumin seeds 1 tsp
Mustard seeds 1 tsp
Black peppercorns 1 tsp
Vodka (for soaking) 1/4 cup
Whole grain mustard 2 tbsp
Fresh dill, finely chopped 1/4 cup
Lemon zest 1 tsp
Optional: Lemon juice Small squeeze
Cheesecloth (to wrap salmon)
Plastic wrap or resealable bag (to contain)

Instructions

  1. Soak the Salmon

    Place salmon in a shallow dish, skin-side down.

    Pour 1/4 cup vodka over the salmon, making sure the flesh is covered.

    Cover and refrigerate for 30 minutes to 1 hour.

    Tip: You’re flavoring and firming the flesh slightly. Don’t soak too long or it’ll get overly tight.

  2. Toast & Crush Spices

    While salmon soaks, toast the seeds:

    Heat fennel, cumin, mustard seeds, and peppercorns in a dry pan until fragrant (1–2 minutes).

    Let cool, then coarsely crush.

  3. Mix Dry Cure

    In a small bowl, combine:

    • Crushed spices
    • 2 tbsp kosher salt
    • 1 tbsp raw sugar

  4. Make Dill-Mustard Paste

    In another bowl, mix:

    • 2 tbsp whole grain mustard
    • 1/4 cup chopped dill
    • 1 tsp lemon zest
    • Optional: squeeze of lemon juice

  5. Assemble the Cure

    Remove salmon from vodka and pat dry.

    Lay cheesecloth on your surface.

    Sprinkle half the dry cure on the cheesecloth where the salmon will sit.

    Place salmon skin-side down.

    Spread the dill-mustard-lemon paste over the flesh side.

    Sprinkle remaining dry cure on top of the paste.

  6. Wrap & Cure

    Wrap tightly in cheesecloth.

    Then wrap the whole thing in plastic wrap or place in a sealed bag.

    Place in a dish, weigh it down with something flat and heavy.

    Refrigerate for 48–72 hours, flipping once per day.

  7. Finish & Slice

    Unwrap salmon. Do not rinse—leave the dill-mustard crust on.

    Pat off any excess moisture.

    Slice thinly at a diagonal, using a sharp knife.

Storage

  • Keep well-wrapped in fridge up to 5 days.
  • Freeze leftovers in slices with parchment between layers.
