When it comes to making pasta dough, mixing flour and eggs can be a rewarding process that results in a homemade, high-quality dish. Here are two methods for combining the ingredients:

Mixing by Hand

To mix the ingredients:

  1. Start by creating a mound of flour on a clean work surface.
  2. Next, make a well in the center to hold the eggs. Adding a splash of olive oil or a pinch of salt is optional.
  3. Gently whisk the eggs with a fork, gradually incorporating flour from the sides.
  4. Once the mixture thickens, knead it by hand for about 8–10 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic.
  5. Finally, wrap the dough in plastic and let it rest for at least 30 minutes to allow the gluten to relax.

Using a Mixer

Using a stand mixer, such as a KitchenAid, simplifies the dough-making process and reduces physical effort. In this method, add the dry ingredients to the bowl with a dough hook attachment, followed by the eggs and any optional ingredients.

The mixer will combine and knead the dough, typically taking 5–7 minutes on a low setting. It's important not to overwork the dough; once it starts to pull away from the sides and forms a ball, it’s ready.

A quick hand-knead for a minute or two can help finish it off before wrapping it up to rest.

Whether you choose to make dough by hand or with a machine, the resting period is crucial. It allows moisture to distribute evenly and the gluten network to relax, making the dough easier to roll out later.

After resting, the dough should feel smooth, supple, and slightly tacky. At that point, you can shape it into a sheet and cut it.
